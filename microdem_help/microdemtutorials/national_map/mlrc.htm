MLRC--NLCD Download

   

Map view and the ability to subset small regions. 

  • https://www.mrlc.gov/viewer/  allows you to subset the data; you want the land cover.  Pick the desired region, and it will be prepped and you will get an email to download with all 8 years.  You may get a larger area than requested (but it will be Albers equal area projection, and you select on a cylindrical projection)
  • You can get all 7 years from 2001 to 2019  of NLCD as a 10.2 GB download.  To open:
    • File, import, GDAL raster  subset to match map extent
    • Load that file in QGIS, and then export a smaller region as a Geotiff for use in MICRODEM.
  • There are two versions.  The science product has two experimental, transitional forest categories (45 and 46).  Unless you need them, don't get them since it just doubles the size of an already large download.

 

Zoom in the to area desired.  The map can be resistant to dragging the viewport; just be persistent.

Open the download tool in the upper right of the map.

 

Pick "Place" in the lower left, which then changes to "clear".

Drag the selection box to where you want it.

Check that the bounding box is correct.  Since you won't see the files for a day, you should be sure you are getting the right area.

Pick land cover, and all land cover years.
 

Keep track of where you saves the file, and being able to determine which data set it is.  The file names for the ZIP, and the extract, is a random string of numbers.  The files should start with NLCD_20NN for the year.  They will be Geotiffs.
