Orbit: 705 km, 10:30 a.m. descending node (Terra) or 1:30 p.m. ascending node (Aqua), sun-synchronous, near-polar, circular

Scan Rate: 20.3 rpm, cross track

Swath Dimensions: 2330 km (cross track) by 10 km (along track at nadir)

Telescope: 17.78 cm diam. off-axis, afocal (collimated), with intermediate field stop

Size: 1.0 x 1.6 x 1.0 m

Weight: 228.7 kg

Power: 162.5 W (single orbit average)

Data Rate: 10.6 Mbps (peak daytime); 6.1 Mbps (orbital average)

Quantization: 12 bits

Spatial Resolution: 250 m (bands 1-2), 500 m (bands 3-7), 1000 m (bands 8-36) Design Life: 6 years (but they launched in 1999 and 2002 and are still going)

VIIRS (NOAA's newest polar orbiter)

  • 16 are moderate resolution bands (M-bands) which have a spatial resolution of 750 m at  nadir
  • 5 bands are imaging resolution bands (I-bands), which have a spatial resolution of 375 m at nadir
  • 1 one day/night panchromatic band with a spatial resolution of 750m.
