Eureka Valley Landslide

Eureka Valley in eastern California, on the edge of Death Valley National Park has a large landslide (Shaller and others, 2020; Petley, 2009; Petley, 2020) that may be similar to features on Mars

https://blogs.agu.org/landslideblog/2023/01/24/blacks-beach-1/

Deliverables:

  • Create a 3D view of the landslide, with the imagery.  Discuss the horizontal and vertical distances covered by the landslide.
  • Digitize the breakaway zone (Qt) and the final runout location (Ql) for the Eureka Valley Landslide.  Submit the shapefiles merged into a single file (use the SHZ option after merging the individual shapefiles or merge as a Google Earth  KMZ file), and discuss how the landslide appears on the 4 available imagery data sets, and why you chose the two that you did to use to digitize.  Insure that you cover the pan and multispectral bands in Landsat.
  • Compare your digitization to the published geologic map and the NAIP imagery
  • Complete the table on the thermal reflectance at 6 locations.  Discuss what affects the differences.

Geologic Map

 Last Chance Range Geologic Quad

  • 4300 x 4300 pixels
  • 22056 x 27865 m
  • 5.12 x 6.48 m/pixel
  • You can overlay this on the DEM, using Overlay, Imagery (KML, KMZ, world files), and picking the KMZ file.  The overlay only works on a map with geographic coordinates; the imagery has UTM coordinates.
  • You can also view the KMZ file in Google Earth, which will have different imagery.
  • As you move you on the map overlaid on the DEM, you will see the locations on the imagery. 
  • You are not to trust the geologic map, but are to outline the landslide on the imagery.
  • Do not try to open the TIF file, or try to overlay the KMZ on any map other than the DEM (it must be overlaid on a map using a geographic lat/long grid).

General digitizing guidance

  • Insure you are working on the full resolution of the raster data, on a big screen
  • Strongly consider a mouse
  • Insure that the entire feature you want to digitize is on screen.  If you try to resize the screen in the middle of digitizing, the program is likely to confuse the two operations.
  • If you cannot get the feature on screen at the full resolution on the data, make it as large as you can, digitize, and then edit the points zoomed in ever farther.
  • Decide if you want point or stream mode (right click, and change from the popup menu)
  • If you use stream mode, set the point tolerance
    • Right click while digitizing, and set tolerance from the popup
  • Some of the digitizing files are saved in temporary storage, and may be deleted the next time you open the program.  If you do not know where the file was saved, save it on the Report menu.
    • Export DBF file if it is points
    • Export shapefile if it is lines or polygons

Digitizing in MICRODEM.  If you are confident of your digitizing ability and doubt you will have to edit your lines.

  • Annotate Map, Create shapefile, area/polygon. 
  • If you were overly optimistic and want to edit the outline, Double click on the record in data base, Export, line/Polygon to point database, and continue with the graphical edits in the step below

Digitizing in MICRODEM if you expect that you might have to edit your lines

  • Annotate Map, Create shapefile, track database.  You cannot add points later, so insure you get enough points for the level of detail you want
  • Once the track (point) table opens, Edit, Graphically move points to drag points to a new location.
  • Convert the track (point) database to a polygon.   Report, Create area/line shape file from points.  You need the polgyon shapefile to compute area.

Digitize in Google Earth. 

  • Open KMZ file of the geologic quad in Google Earth to locate the Last Chance Range and Eureka Valley
  • To add the ortho image to Google Earth, 
    • Subset & zoom
    • 1:1 map view to insure you have the full resolution of the imagery
    • Right click, Export, Quick map to Google Earth
  • Digitize points (if you want lines/polygons, you will have to install DNRGPS and use that to convert a shapeilf)
  • Save as a KML file
  • Database, and open the KML file to get it converted to a DBF file
  • If you have points, Report, Create area/line shape file from points

Digitize in Google Earth with your imagery

  • Area computation requires the polygons from your digitization

Create a 3D view

  • Open DEM
  • Open image you want to use
  • Subset & zoom the image to just the area you want
  • Make the map big (the quality of the view is directly related to its size)
  • OpenGL 3D view from the image toolbar

Merging Files

  • Merge shapefiles:  You will probably have 4 files with the outline of the landslide (two different images, and separate files for the two regions, and if you have both on screen you probably are not seeing any details).  Separate shapefiles can make symbolization and creation of a legend easier, but you will combine them for submisson.  Insure the name field shows if it is Ql or Qt, and the source imagery used.
  • Merge KMZ: KML: export for Google earth, for all the files in Google Earth, move them all to be the only things in a single  folder, and then save that folder as a KMZ.    Clean up KML exports and save KML/KMZ
