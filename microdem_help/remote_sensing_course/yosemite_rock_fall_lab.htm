Lab 4--Yosemite Rock Fall

Deliverables

  • Discuss whether the lidar data is good enough for a 0.5 m grid, a 1 m grid, or a 2 m grid.  Support your choice with a point density grid.
  • Discuss which DEM you prefer (NVS, DTM, DSM), and why.
  • Discuss the utility or lack thereof for the intensity and classification grids.  Is this a general assessment, or specific to these two data sets.
  • Prepare a high quality map showing where the rockfall started, and where it came to rest.  You want this map to show as much of the story as you can tell on a single map.
  • Get a topographic profile of the cliff surface before and after the landslide
  • Compute the change in volume at both locations.  Does the volume change at the headwall match that where the slide came to rest?

In steep terrain, slight horizontal mis-registations can lead to large differences in elevation.  This frequently leads to linear large differences in elevation along the sides of buildings, for example.  You should be suspicious of such apparent changes, and lend more credence to differences over a large area.
