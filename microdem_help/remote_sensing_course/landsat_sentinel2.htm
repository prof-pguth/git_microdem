Sentinel-2 scenes come in 100 km tiles, named for the UTM 100k squares which have a two letter designation in the MGRS system.    Since the swath width of the sensor is 290 km, each day's pass will be in multliple (3-4) tiles.  The two images here show scenes before and after fires in southern Australia.  On the scene to the left, post fire, the western part of the tile was not imaged in this orbit.  The earlier image, before the fires, completely covered this tile



For Landsat, each pass of the satellite (call a path) is broken in rows which are approximately square but rotated by the inclination of the orbit.

For both systems, the compressed files for each scene are arbout 1GB, and decompress to about 2 GB.
