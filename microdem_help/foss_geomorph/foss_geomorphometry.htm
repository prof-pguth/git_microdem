FOSS Geomorphometry

A workshop, presented in October 2013 at Geomorphometry 2013 in Nanjing, China, provided an introduction to Free and Open Source Software for Geomorphometry.  Some people will insist and on both "free" and "open source" modifying each program, but we will take a more expansive view and include both "freeware" and "open source".  Freeware is software that is free to use, but does not include source code. 

This workshop builds on

  • Software descriptions in the book edited by Hengl and Reuter (2009) which included 8 software packages, two commercial and 6 free.  We will only briefly mention the commercial ArcGIS, because it is the de facto standard GIS program, and will not discuss the commercial program RiverTools.
  • Portable GIS, GIS on a USB stick from Jo Cook (not updated since 2016), which includes one of the packages (GRASS and QGIS front end).  To that we have added 4 additional packages (SAGA, ILWIS, MICRODEM, and Whitebox Tools) included in Hengl and Reuter (2009).   LandSerf is not being actively maintained, and there are problems getting it to run with current versions of Java, so it will not be considered.

Why not one single free GIS program?

  • Language choice (Java, C/C++, Delphi)
  • Historical development and internal data models not compatible
  • Focus of programs differ
