Right Click Physical Geography

On a map window, right clicking, Physical geography provides these options:

  • Tissot
  • All climate graphs
  • Multiple climate graphs
  • Monthly insolation
  • Monthly temperature ranges
  • Evapotranspiration/precipitation--the water budget
  • Monthly wind
  • Koppen climograph from climate grids
  • Sun position equinox/solstices
  • Annual length daylight
  • Annual sunrise/sunset.  This provides
    • Latitude, based on the difference in daylight from summer to  winter
    • Hemisphere, based on the timing of max (summer) and min (winter) daylight
  • Annual solar elevation
    • Elevation to thesun at local noon (not exactly noon, depending on position with the time zone, and possible daylight savings time
  • Moon rise/set (Montenbruck and Pfleger 1991): table with sun and moon rise and settting times, and a second table with the moon phases for the next year
  • Moon position (USNO web site)--requires web connection, and will include a single day from midnight to midnight
    • Today
    • Pick date
    • Overlay today or selected day
  • NOAA weather forecast (US)
  • Terrain sun blocking
  • Key latitudes
  • Quick rotate map (must be a planar projection)

Julian days start with 1 for January 1, and goes to 365 or 366 for December 31.
