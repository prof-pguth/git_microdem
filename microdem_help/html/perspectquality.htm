Perspective Image Quality

Image quality with a draped perspective depends on the following:

  • Size of the window. Bigger is generally better, but slower, until you get to the point where you don't have enough data to support the requirements.
  • Vertical exaggeration in the window. You can change this by resizing the window, or changing the maximum and minimum viewport angles.
  • Spacing of points on the radials. The default should give optimal image quality; you might consider reducing this spacing for faster display.
  • Distance out from the observer. The close in points will only have a few data points spread across the entire screen. You can avoid this problem by raising the minimum vertical angle, or increasing the distance to the first point that will be considered. The second option might result in unrealistic views, by ignoring hills close to your location and letting you see behind them, but the image quality will be better.
  • Max size of the drape image. Set on Views tab of options form.

The program will redraw the draped image map with pixel size equal to the DEM data spacing or the image data spacing before performing the view. However, if the max size of the drape image does not allow drawing with this pixel size, you will get decreased resolution.

For example:

  • 1 m imagery, covering an area of 25 x 25 km.
  • Max drape size to 2000 x 2000 pixels.
  • View requires a drape map that is 10 km by 10 km.
  • To get the full resolution from your 1 m imagery would require 10,000 x 10,000 pixels. To get within the limitsof 2000 x 2000, the drape map will have a resolution of 5 m.
  • To get better resolution, you could:
    • Try increasing the size of the drape maps, although your hardware and the OS might not support this.
    • Decrease the depth of the view. If your drape map only required 5 x 5 km, the resolution would be 2.5 m. To get the full value from the 1 m imagey, you would have to make a view only 2 x 2 km.
    • You will only get the benefit of the the high resolution very close to the viewer's position; farther back the extra resolution will be "wasted".
