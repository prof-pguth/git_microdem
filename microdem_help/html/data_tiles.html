Data Tiles

Many GIS data sets are tiles, broken in small rectangles.  This commonly applies to point clouds in LAS format, DEM grids, orthoimagery, and maps delivered over the internet.

This has a number of advantages:

  • Only the tiles that are on the map need to be opened and processed.
  • Speed, with several scales of tiles stored, and the appropriate tiles are loaded and scaled for the screen.
  • Speed, for operations like Delaunay triangulation where the algorithms are most efficent for a small number of points.

On a slow connection, you can see the tiling in Google Earth, as the map appears tile by tile.
