TOPEX Orbits

Ascending passes have the altimeter satellite moving from the vicinity of the south pole northward; they travel from SE to NW. For the TOPEX/Poseidon, they have odd numbers.

Descending passes have the altimeter satellite moving from the vicinity of the north pole southward; they travel from NE to SW. For the TOPEX/Poseidon, they have even numbers.

Two passes make up an orbit, and the TOPEX/Poseidon satellite has 254 passes or 127 orbits per 10 day cycle.

Map shows the coverage of the TOPEX/Poseidon radar altimeter, but all polar orbiting satellites would show a similar geometry. The coverage of all 254 passes is indicated with the gray color, with the first eight highlighted. Note that each orbit steps westward from the preceding orbit, and that the intervening area will be filled in much later in the cycle.
