Google Earth Data Layers

This is a very small selection of what is currently available.  We have no intentions of trying to keep it current or comprehensive.

  • Geology
  • Tides and currents, NOAA: http://tidesandcurrents.noaa.gov/googleearth.shtml, including
    • Sea level trends
    • Tide predictions from the Google Earth screen
  • USGS, multiple files: http://earthquake.usgs.gov/learn/kml.php
  • Piracy, NGA ASAM: http://msi.nga.mil/NGAPortal/MSI.portal?_nfpb=true&_pageLabel=msi_portal_page_65 (download and unzip and open in Google Earth)
  • Hazards: http://www.ngdc.noaa.gov/hazard/
  • Hurricanes: http://www.nhc.noaa.gov/gis/
  • Global energy: http://globalenergyobservatory.org/select.php?tgl=Kml
  • Lightning: http://flash3.ess.washington.edu/WWLLN.kml
  • Rainfall: http://pmm.nasa.gov/data-access/google-earth
  • Snow analysis: http://www.nohrsc.noaa.gov/earth/
  • Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (worldwide, monthly): http://ti.arc.nasa.gov/m/project/planetary/kml/ndvi.kmz
  • Environmental issues: http://earth.tryse.net/
  • Weather:
    • http://wdssii.nssl.noaa.gov/
