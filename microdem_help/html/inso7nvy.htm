Earth Viewed from the Sun

June solstice: the sun is overhead at the Tropic of Cancer (23½°N), and the North Pole and all points north of the Arctic Circle (66½°N) face the sun for 24 hours a day. Points south of the Antarctic Circle (66½°S) do not see the sun during a 24 hour period.
Equinox (March or September): the sun is directly overhead at the equator, and all points on the earth see the sun for 12 hours.
December solstice: the sun is overhead at the Tropic of Capricorn (23½°S), and the South Pole and all points south of the Antarctic Circle (66½°S) face the sun for 24 hours a day. Points north of the Arctic Circle (66½°N) do not see the sun during a 24 hour period.
