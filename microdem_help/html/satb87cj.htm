Standard statistics can be applied to the pixel DNs within a scene. These include the average (mean), standard deviation, and the minimum and maximum DNs present in the scene. These statistics can be viewed graphically in histograms.

The minimum and maximum values present depend on whether any pixels within the scene reflect the energies that correspond to those DNs. For reflected energy this will depend on the highest and lowest albedos present in the area, and the insolation present. The highest DN requires high insolation with high albedo; the lowest DN could be some combination of low insolation and low albedo.

The standard deviation determines the range of DNs present in the scene. The larger the standard deviation, the more contrast.
