Launched on July 14, 2022, and installed on the International Space Station, EMIT is an imaging spectrometer that records visible to short-wave infrared (VSWIR) light reflected from Earth's surface. During its planned one-year mission, EMIT will collect data over Earth’s dust-source regions—namely, the arid regions of Africa, Asia, North and South America, and Australia—between 52° north and south latitude. With a viewing swath of 75 kilometers, 285 spectral bands, and a spatial resolution of 60 meters, the data EMIT collects will be used to produce mineral maps to determine the sources of dust in the atmosphere. (Meet EMIT, the Newest Imaging Spectrometer | Earthdata (nasa.gov))
