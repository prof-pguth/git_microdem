Map of Monthly Climate Parameters

Multiple ways to open

  • Physical Geography labs Option on file menu
  • File menu, Open
  • Climate options on a map toolbar, then Monthly climatology

These are stored as a series of grids, one for each month, or as a vector database.  As such, they behave slightly differently than normal grids:

  • You can cycle rapidly through the annual cycle in map view.
  • Monthly graph at point for a single monthly climate parameter by right clicking on the map.
  After opening one parameter, you can open others from the monthly animation form with the "Load another" button.

The open data sets will be on the right, with an indication if they are raster grids or vector databases.  Turning these on can make changing the display faster, and easier to see the other data sets.

Pick the month, or use the < and > buttons to cycle through the year.

For vector databases, you can thin the data before plotting, which will be faster, and may not degrade the image quality.

You can get the annual sum for a parameter like precipitation, or the annual mean for a parameter like temperature.   These will show the same patterns, differing only by a factor of 12, but depending on the parameter, one might have units that allow easier interpretation.

You can get a map of min, max, and range of a parameter through the year.

You can hide or show legends.

 

Several parameters can be opened together, and provide particular graphs:

  • Evapotranspiration/precipitation, to show the water budget. 
  • Temperature and precipitation, which lets you get Kopppen classification
  • Daily temperature range

If you want you can open the individual monthly grids you can use standard procedures for opening DEMs/grids.

Calculate Menu, Average by latitude with the desired grid the active map, can compute how the parameter varies by latitude, for monthly values, or the annual sum or mean.
