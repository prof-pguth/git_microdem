Satellite Band Tradeoffs

A band is a portion of the electromagnetic spectrum sensed by a satellite.  You can think of it as a color, but often some of the bands will be in the infrared and invisible to the human eye.

To collect a lot of energy, the band must be wide,covering a large range of frequencies or wavelengths and hence a range of colors.  The designers of a satellite consider tradeoffs. The response of the sensor is also not perfect with absolute boxcar edges.

The Sentinel-2 satellite shows one response to these demands

https://www.usgs.gov/faqs/what-are-band-designations-landsat-satellites?qt-news_science_products=0#qt-news_science_products
  • Note that for the RGB bands 2-4, the Sentinel-2 satellite has 10 m resolution but relatively large bandwidth.
  • Band 8, in the NIR, also has 10 m spatial resolution but a much larger bandwidth, in part because there is less reflected energy in the IR.
  • There is a second NIR Band, 8A, with 20 m spatial resolution and narrower bandwidth.
  • A number of bands (1, 5, 6, 7, 9, 10) have very narrow bandwidth because they are looking for a particular absorption pattern, but have to use lower spatial resolution.
