Stereo Net Statistics

Calculate and display statistics for the data currently plotted on the stereo net.

The average orientation is calculated using formulas given in Davis (1986, p.331-334). As noted there (Davis, 1986, p.334), it will NOT work if the vectors are widely dispersed. In that case you should use eigen analysis. The calculated parameters include:

  • n, the number of data points
  • dip and dip direction of the average vector
  • alpha (alpha95), the angular uncertaintly of the average
  • R, the strength of the result. Values close to 1 are best.

The average is computed by summing the x, y, and z components of all vectors and divided each sum by the number of observations. The orientation of the resulting vector is then determined. The length of the resulting vector is the R parameter.

Eigen analysis follows Davis (1986, p.334-339) or Fisher and others (1987). Note that it may provide a better estimate for the mean orientation.
