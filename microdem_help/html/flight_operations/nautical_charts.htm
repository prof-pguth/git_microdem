Nautical Charts

 

Vector Data--NOAA has changed much of this

  • DNC (Digital Nautical Chart)--download for the US, convert VPF to shape files in GDAL.
  • NOAA ENC--direct to GIS convert to shape files at the NOAA website
    • https://encdirect.noaa.gov/help/encdirect_help.html#frequently-asked-questions
  • Mobile Offshore Drilling Units (MODU) http://msi.nga.mil/NGAPortal/MSI.portal?_nfpb=true&_pageLabel=msi_portal_page_67  in shapefile or KMZ
  • Broadcast warnings: http://msi.nga.mil/NGAPortal/MSI.portal?_nfpb=true&_pageLabel=msi_portal_page_63 as a KMZ file
  • Download NOAA S57 files
    • Use GDAL  to convert to shapefiles (FWTools0.9.5> ogr2ogr -skipfailures out us5tx51m.000--out is a direction, the .000 is the S57 file)

NOAA chart on demand

  • https://devgis.charttools.noaa.gov/pod/  makes PDF chart with control of size, scale, and coverage area

Raster Data--NOAA is discontinuing raster charts

  • Online viewer: http://www.nauticalcharts.noaa.gov/mcd/NOAAChartViewer.html
  • Historical NOAA nautical charts
  • MICRODEM can display raster charts converted to Geotiff format.  
  • NOAA nautical charts can now be downloaded in BSB, a proprietary format from Maptech, at
    • http://www.nauticalcharts.noaa.gov/mcd/Raster/download_agreement.htm
    • http://www.freeboatingcharts.com/
  • Convert BSB files for use in MICRODEM using the converter from NOAA, available at http://www.csc.noaa.gov/digitalcoast/tools/chartreprojector (or from the data files download in MICRODEM)
    • After you download and unzip the chart, you must select the KAP file to convert.
    • Geographic will probably give the best results in MICRODEM.
    • UTM also works.
  • You can also convert the KAP file by opening an image (the .KAP file) and letting GDAL convert.  If it does not display, you must then run GDAL_warp on the Geotiff created.  Making this one stop is on our to do list.

Point Soundings

  • NOAA soundings:
    • http://maps.ngdc.noaa.gov/viewers/bathymetry/ . 
      • Turn on data in "NOS Hydrographic Surveys". 
      • Zoom in on map.
      • Outline box
      • Pick a survey by its ID,
      • Get data in XYZ format, Decompress the GZ file, and convert to a DBF file. May have to open in Excel, and save as a CSV file, before the file will open in MICRODEM.
      • BAG files will have the best data, for small regions
        • Can request conversion of BAG file to XYZ.
        • Can extract bands with GDAL, and then open the band with the depths (the second band has the uncertainty).  Have to edit out the missing data, and multiply depths by -1.
        • Grids will be huge, and may be almost entirely missing data with small "islands" of data.  You can subset and save just the areas with data.
    • http://maps.ngdc.noaa.gov/viewers/nos_hydro/ Alternative link.
