1:24,000 USGS DEM--Historical Interest Only

The 1:24K DEM has points every 30 m on a UTM grid based on NAD-27. Data spacing is regular, but because of the construction of the UTM grid in 6° zones the grid is rotated with respect to geographic latitude-longitude coordinates. DEMs cover 7.5 minute topographic map quadrangles, and elevation profiles are clipped at the boundaries of the quadrangle, which creates complexities not present in the 1:250K DEM. Grid north-true north divergence increases away from the central meridian in each zone, leading to variable numbers of columns and rows in the DEMs due both to the UTM grid and convergence of the meridians with latitude. Figures in the table were calculated by computing the UTM of quadrangle corners at various latitudes, and various distances from a typical central meridian (117°W). The calculations cover the extreme cases at the east and west margins of the UTM zone, where the UTM grid is rotated the greatest distance from the meridians of longitude, as well as the central cases where rotation is the least. The numbers in the table contain a small safety margin, and the actual number of columns and rows in the data sets I have worked with was 1-2 less than that predicted. Along the edges of the UTM zone the number of rows in the data sets increase to a maximum of 477, from 463-467 along the central meridian. The number of columns also increases somewhat moving away from the central meridian, but that effect is much less than the effect of the converging meridians moving north.
