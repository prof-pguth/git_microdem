Classify Point Clouds

Classifying point clouds looks at the returns in a region, and attempts to assign them to a category based on geometric properties of the points.  The resulting classification can be stored in the LAS file.

  • ground: finds the ground returns, which define a continuous surface at the bottom of the point cloud, excluding low noise.  This requires several parameters, most importantly a step size which affects how buildings will be excluded, and a bulge parameter which affects how much local variation is allowed in the surface created by a TIN of the ground points. 
  • roofs: regions above the ground with planar orientation.  There are generally few returns from the sides of buildings.
  • Vegetation: regions above the ground with rough surface, and multiple points within the canopy.  Success will depend on the number of points; the more mulitple returns, the easier it is to differentiate the trees. 
  • water: very flat, horizontal surfaces, with low intensity.  May only see water with very small scan angles

Options to peform Ground point classification in MICRODEM.  These are not designed for robust use, but as educational tools to show how the process works.
