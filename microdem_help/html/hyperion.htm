Hyperion

  • Hyperion flew on Earth Observing-1 (EO-1) satellite from 2000 to 2017.  The satellite also had the multispectral ALI instrument, which contributed to the development of Landsat 8.
  • Hyperion provides a high resolution hyperspectral imager capable of resolving 242 dataspectral bands (from 0.4 to 2.5 µm) with a 30-meter resolution. The instrument can image a 7.5 km by 100 km land area per image, and provide detailed spectral mapping across all channels with high radiometric accuracy.
  • Bands B001-B007, B058-B076, and B225-242 are not calibrated, and have no valid values, so 198 bands have useful data.

Getting archived Hyperion data

  • https://eo1.usgs.gov/documents/Hyperion_L1G_EO1-DFCB.v.1.pdf
  • From EarthExplorer, under EO-1
  • Get L1Gst Product in GeoTiff format, in the additional criteria
  • Path and Row cover a large area, and each image covers only a very small part of it
  • Unzip files.  Use the option to unzip into the current directory, which will put each into its own folder becasue the zips actually zip up a folder.
  • When opening, you need to select the directory with a name like "EO1H0410342015285110K0" and not the one that ends with "1GST"
