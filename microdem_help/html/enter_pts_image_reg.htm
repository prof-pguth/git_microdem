Enter points for image registration

  1. Enter coordinates either from the keyboard,  by using a second registered image or map, or from Google Earth.  To use another image, load it after you select the image you will be registering.  You could  use any other map, like a TIGER county map.  If you use another map (or enter coordinates from the keyboard), the accuracy of your registration depends on the accuracy of the coordinates that you read from the other map.  You can get the map pixel size with the Information on the map from the map toolbar. The accuracy will be no better than the pixel size on the map you use to get coordinates, and the actual accuracy of the original data.
  2. Insure that the datum is correct, and you have the correct UTM zone.
  3. Repeat the following steps at least three times:
    • Click the "Image Point" button, and then double click on a location on the image you want to register.
    • Click the "Map Point" button, and then double click on the same location in the registered image.  Alternatively, click on "Enter coords" to use the keyboard.
    • Click the "Next Point" button.
  4. You can enter points from Google Earth using "KML points".  This can provide very accurate positions using the Google Earth imagery.
    • You work on the last point in the file.
    • Click the "Image Point" button, and then double click on a location on the image you want to register.
    • Double click on a record in the table, and click the option to move it to the end of the file, where you can enter its image coordinates.
  5. When done, you can do a "Reg File" to create the registration file.

This is part of Image Registration.
