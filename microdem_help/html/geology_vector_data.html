Other Databases.

  • Plates Project, University of Texas (http://www-udc.ig.utexas.edu/external/plates/data.htm).
    • Large Igneous Provinces. Converted from a GMT file. Large igneous provinces (LIPs) are voluminous emplacements of predominantly mafic extrusive and intrusive rock whose origins lie in processes other than 'normal' seafloor spreading. LIPs include continental flood basalts and associated intrusive rocks, volcanic passive margins, oceanic plateaus, submarine ridges, seamount groups, and ocean basin flood basalts.
