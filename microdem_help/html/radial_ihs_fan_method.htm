Radials, Full Coverage

Version 14.33 (September 2002) introduced a new viewshed algorithm.   It was originally called the Radial IHS Fan Method.

The radial spacing and the distance along the radials are selected so that the fan area will be completely filled in. Along the radials there will be coverage at the half the spacing of the DEM or the map (whichever is less), unless you override the default. There may be gaps between radials if the map is not displayed at 1:1 map view, but the faster computation may be worth this tradeoff. Go faster will less accuracy.

This method provides very dense coverage near the observer (weapon) location, because radials have a constant angular separation.  Many calculations may be done for the same pixel, and it may be colored both visible and masked.  This indicates that portions of the pixel differ in their visibility, and depending on how you want to consider the analysis, you should request either the visible or the masked option.

This method will be much faster than the Point to point method (once called IHS merge), which calculates intervisibility from every point on the map to the observer.  Because intervisibility is cumulative, but no map points are on exactly the same line (except the rare case of the N-S and E-W lines), there is a tremendous amount of duplication.  For small screen regions this is not noticeable, but for large fans it slows down the calculations.  Because this method goes out only along the radials there is much less duplication of computations.

Viewshed algorithms lists the pros and cons of this and other algorithms.
