Delaunay Triangulation Registration

  For quadrangle registration, the registration points lie on the ticks (or graticule intersections), and the triangles subdivide each rectangle.  There are two potential ways to draw the triangles, and random effects (slight errors in locating points, and imprecision in floating point computations) determine which is drawn.  This will not affect the results.

You can note any missing registration points on this diagram, enter them, and then redo the triangulation registration.

 
  With Delaunay triangulation, you will get graphs of the Lat and Long versus the image coordinates.  This should both be close to linear; if not, the graph pinpoints likely blunders in the digitization process.  The points will not be entirely linear if you can see bending of the parallels and meridians on the map, or if the map is not perfectly aligned in the image.

You can correct any problem registration points pointed out on this diagram, and then redo the triangulation registration.  You can identify the problem points from their image coordinates and geographic coordinates, find them in the registration point database, and correct them.

  Fix problems on the image registration form
  • edit the coordinates of the initial corners in the map by typing into the grid,
  • re-select the Quad map.
  • Verify the grid/tick spacing
  • Say yes to recompute the interior points
  • Add any missing points by selecting "Next point", and then double clicking on the point.
  • If there are extra points, you can double click on them in the grid display, and pick delete registration point.
  • Redo the Reg file, TIN mapping
