Key Remote Sensing Options in MICRODEM

Imagery analysis menu  on map window.  In addition to looking at the satellite image and treating it like a photograph for visual interpretation, a number of statistical operations can be performed with the digital data. These include:

  • Contrast Enhancement. 
  • Digital filtering: digital filtering can smooth or enhance differences in the image. 
  • Reflectance histograms: graphs of DN versus the number of pixels with that DN. Several bands often have a strong bimodal distribution, with water as one of the modes.
  •  Correlation (Covariance) Matrix. Create a covariance matrix.
  •  Principal Components: Compute Principal components.
  • Reflectance scattergrams: graph of the reflectance in one band versus the reflectance in another band.
  • Statistics: basic measures like mean, standard deviation, minimum and maximum.
  • Ratio images: the ratios of reflectances in two bands can better differentiate surface categories than looking at a single image alone.  This can do a good job of differentiating land and water, or vegetation, despite differences like shadows or differential lighting.
  • For Landsat, several normalized satellite indexes are commonly used.  
  • Multiband merge.
  • Training and supervised classification:  divides the image into categories.
    • Supervised classification.
    • Unsupervised classification.
  • Overlay menu, 2 maps, vertical swipe compare

Many of the imagery analysis options require conversion of the data to multiple grids.  This converts them to the same format as DEMs, and allows the same code to operate on both.
