Register Scanned Quadrangle Map With Gazetteer

  Daily situation map from World War II, available from the Library of Congress web site.

Registering historic MrSid Map scans

This is a conical orthomorphic projection, which we are not going to reverse engineer.

There is a ½º lat/long grid in the center of the map used for a quad registration .

  • The four corner points on the graticule were digitized, then an affine registration (Rotation registration) provided a rough registration.
  • The remaining points were quickly entered with the quad registration option.  
  • These points are unlabeled in the center of the map.

The remaining points on the margins, where there are no lat/long points indicated, were entered from the gazetteer.  These points are labeled along the map margins.

When done, perform Reg File and then the TIN Mapping option.
  The program creates a Delaunay triangulation.

You can also extrapolate to the edges of the map after you do the Delaunay triangulation, and then retriangulate.

There are two potential ways to draw the triangles, and random effects (slight errors in locating points, and perhaps also imprecision in floating point computations) determine which is drawn.

You can get locations anywhere within the triangles.
 

Export and tile map for Google Earth.
 

This example does not consider datum shifts.  The maps do not indicate the datum used, and at the map scale (each pixel is about 36 m) and with allowable cartographic license, it was not worth considering.  That additional step could be used if warranted.
