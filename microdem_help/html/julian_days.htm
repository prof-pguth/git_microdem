Julian Days

Julian date has two distinct usages, depending on where your roots are:

  • Julian day is the continuous count of days since the beginning of the Julian Period used primarily by astronomers.
  • An ordinal date is a calendar date typically consisting of a year and a day of year ranging between 1 and 366 (starting on January 1), though year may sometimes be omitted.  With the year omitted, this can be used to schedule periodic maintenance, or to plot annual cycles in the earth sciences
