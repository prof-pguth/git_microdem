Geomorphic Curvature

Curvature is the rate of change of slope, so it is the second derivative of the elevation surface.

Computing in MICRODEM

  • External tools has a number of curvature computations; I do not vouch for their correct operation, especially for arc second DEMs.
    • GRASS
    • SAGA
    • Whitebox
  • Derivative grid. can compute curvature.  They will be drawn with a percentile color stretch.  
    • Profile (kns)
    • Plan (kpc)
    • Tangential (knc)
    • Flow line (rotor, kps)
    • Contour torsion (tc)
    • Maximum (k_max)
    • Mean (k_mean)
    • Minimum (k_min)
    • Gaussian (k)
    • Casorati (kc)

Minar and others (2020) 

  • reviewed the state of curvature.  Units were a mess, positive/negative didn't agree, and scaling was inconsistent (some implementations tried to avoid really small values by multiplying by a constant). 
  •  listed a total of 25 curvatures, and three basic (trio of curvatures):
    • Profile
    • Plan.  Wilson (2018) followed Mitasova and Hofierka, 1993, and Gallant and Wilson, 2000) in suggesting that tangential curvature (plan curvature multiplied by the sine of the slope angle) as being more appropriate due to the behaviour of plan curvature at low slope angles)
    • Twisting (rotor in Whitebox)

Parametric isotropic smoothing: recommended by Hengl and Evans (2008, after Sharry and others, 2002) before computing curvature or other derivatives.  Use this (or any smoothing) with caution, as it will reduce roughness which you might want.
