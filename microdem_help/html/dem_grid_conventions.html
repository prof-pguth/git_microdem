The first widely available DEMs were created by US mapping agencies.  The military (NGA/NIMA/DMA depending on when you are talking about) and civil (USGS) went separate ways.  Their decisions still affect the Copernicus DEM in the 2020s.

USGS National Mapping Program, 1992-1998, Technical Instructions, Standards for Digital Elevation Models

DEM starts at the SW corner, with rows running south to north, and columns running west to east.  Both in the figures and text, the “elevation points” represent a point with no discussion about areas.

NGA/NIMA/DMA

National Imagery and Mapping Agency, 2000, MIL-PRF-89020B, PERFORMANCE SPECIFICATION: DIGITAL TERRAIN ELEVATION DATA.

  •  DEM starts at the SW corner, with rows running south to north, and columns running west to east
  •  Values are “true elevation of point”
  •  Thinning done by decimation; the elevations in the 3” product are the same as those in the corresponding position in the 1” product
