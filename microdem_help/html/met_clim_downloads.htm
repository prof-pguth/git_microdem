Weather and Climate Data Downloads

Climate Weather

Climate records, data tables and graphs: 

  • Chelsa: https://chelsa-climate.org/downloads/  (1 arc second grids)
    • Online climographs: https://chelsa-climate.org/climate-diagrams/
  • https://www.ncdc.noaa.gov/cdo-web/
  • Consolidated US government climate data: https://www.data.gov/climate/
  • WMO, worldwide station data: ftp://ftp.atdd.noaa.gov/pub/GCOS/WMO-Normals
  • ICOADS: http://rda.ucar.edu/datasets/ds540.0/  International Comprehensive Ocean-Atmosphere Data Set (ICOADS) Release 2.5, Individual Observations
  • http://iridl.ldeo.columbia.edu/ 
  • http://www.weather.gov/climate/index.php
  • ESA CCI (Climate change initiative): ESA Climate Change Initiative
  • FAO Aquastat Potential evapotranspiration
  • http://www.esrl.noaa.gov/psd/data/reanalysis/reanalysis.shtml can give monthly fields of weather data (such as wind vectors)
  • http://www.esrl.noaa.gov/psd/data/gridded/
  • Koppen
  • NASA Earth Observatory: https://earthobservatory.nasa.gov/global-maps can download Geotiffs, data from 2000-2020
  • CRU_CL_2_0: New, M., Lister, D., Hulme, M. and Makin, I., 2002: A high-resolution data set of surface climate over global land areas. Climate Research 21:1-25
  • http://koeppen-geiger.vu-wien.ac.at/shifts.htm: Current climate and shifts 1900 to 2100, 0.5 minute grid.    Rubel, F., and M. Kottek, 2010: Observed and projected climate shifts 1901-2100 depicted by world maps of the Köppen-Geiger climate classification. Meteorol. Z., 19, 135-141. DOI: 10.1127/0941-2948/2010/0430.
  • WorldClim: Global grids of 7 parameters, 30" to 10' spacing, both for the current world (1970-2000; https://www.worldclim.org/data/worldclim21.html) and future CMIP6 projections (4 20 year time periods out to 2100;  https://www.worldclim.org/data/cmip6/cmip6climate.html
  • ftp://ftp.ncdc.noaa.gov/pub/data/normals/   1961-1990 Climate Normals
  • PRISM grids from Oregon State
  • CHIRPS:
    • 0.05 degree grids, 50N to 50S,
    • 1981 to present
    • daily to annual
    • https://www.chc.ucsb.edu/data/chirps
    • http://chg-wiki.geog.ucsb.edu/wiki/CHIRPS_FAQ  
  • Global maps of a dozen or so parameters, can download in Geotiff (0.1 to 1 degree resolution) http://earthobservatory.nasa.gov/GlobalMaps/?eocn=topnav&eoci=globalmaps
  • Climate and economic data: http://www.wri.org/our-work/project/cait-climate-data-explorer
  • CMIP5 Global Climate Change Viewer  http://regclim.coas.oregonstate.edu/gccv/
  • US Climate normals
    • https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/products/us-climate-normals
    • https://ncei-normals-mapper.rcc-acis.org/

National Weather Service Forecasts

  • KML: https://www.wpc.ncep.noaa.gov/kml/kmlproducts.php

Hurricane GIS data

  • Best source, GIS formats, current weather: http://www.nhc.noaa.gov/gis/
  • http://weather.unisys.com/hurricane/atlantic/ 
  • http://weather.unisys.com/hurricane/ 
  • http://coast.noaa.gov/hurricanes/?redirect=301ocm  (web maps, download button at upper right; none of the formats are ideal for GIS)
  • USGS hurricane: http://coastal.er.usgs.gov/hurricanes/index.html
  • NOAA hurricane center: http://www.nhc.noaa.gov/ (especially the historical information)
  • http://www.aoml.noaa.gov/hrd/tcfaq/tcfaqG.html (G11 to G14)

Tornadoes, Hail, Wind (1950's on)

  • http://www.spc.noaa.gov/gis/svrgis/   (also http://www.spc.noaa.gov/wcm/#data )

Snow

  • Snow coverage--KML files
  • https://www.globsnow.info/  data in Matlab and netcdf, which can be opened in QGIS and converted to Geotiff
GLOBAL GWTC (GWTC-2)--https://www.personal.kent.edu/~cclee/gwtc2global.html : daily weather in 11 categories at 0.5 degree resolution in an ASCII file
