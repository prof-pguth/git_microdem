Rose Diagram of Focal Plane Dip Directions

Example of a section along the Mid Atlantic ridge.  There are 29 earthquakes, leading to 58 focal planes.

Rose diagrams, strikes.  The E-W strikes are the  transform faults, and the N-S strikes are the normal faults.
Rose diagrams, dip directions.  The northward dips are the strike slip faults (which are all nearly vertical), and eastward and westward dips are the normal faults.

 Filter the results by fault type to show just the normal or the transform faults.

Get the orientations on the stereo net:

  • Focal planes on stereonet
  • Poles to focal planes on stereonet
  • Dip directions for focal planes on stereonet

From the dip directions, you could look for fault scarp.
