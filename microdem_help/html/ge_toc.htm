Google Earth Table of Contents

Google Earth follows the GIS model of dividing the map into layers.

The table of contents on the left side of Google Earth shows the map layers.  You can check/uncheck them to turn of layers, and toggle between the map layer, and the underlying imagery or other layers.  Your layers could appear in either "My Places" or "Temporary Places".

Use the small triangles to expand or contract sublayers.

Drag layers, including  sub-elements, to another location.

Add new folders to organize the map.

To delete a layer, right click on it, and pick "Delete".

To save a layer, right click on it, and pick "Save place as".  You can save folders and all layers under them.  It is best to save a KMZ rather than KML (less disk space, and it keeps all files together).

To change the symbolization of a layer, right click on it, Pick "Properties", and then select the "Style, Color" tab.  You can change symbols one by one (slow and inefficient) or agree to change all the symbols in the layer.

The bottom section has layers, which you may want to remove from the map to avoid clutter, and you might want to minimize the display of those layers,  especially if you have a small screen:

  • Borders and labels
  • Photos
  • Roads

While you can do some naming of the layers when you export from MICRODEM, it can be just as easy to right click and select the "Rename" option in Google Earth.

If you create a time animation from a database,   you can drag a base map into the folder created for a particular time step.
