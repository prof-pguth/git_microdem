Gulf of California Triple Junction

Triple junction motion rules

A.   Motions of the three plates in a reference frame, superimposed on the map. Note that the triple junction is not depicted as a single point, as the Cocos plate is in the process of being subducted and broken into two fragments.
B.  Vector diagram of the three plate motions

C.  Triple junction motions.  Solid lines are the velocity vectors between each pair of plates, thin lines the possible motion of the triple junction.  Green is the Cocos-Pacific ridge, cyan is the Pacific North America transform, and blue is the North America trench, with the triple junction motion attached to the overriding North American plate.  The three thin lines do not intersect in a point, so as drawn the triple juction would not be stable.  Possible changes that could make it stable:

  • The orientation of the trench in not correct.  You must set that, and if you get it wrong, the results will also be wrong.  You should also check that you have the correct plate subducting.
  • The spreading could not be symmertrical (faster for one plate compared to the other), and it could also be slightly oblique.

With minor adjustment, the stable triple junction does not move relative to North America, but moves relative to the two other plates.
