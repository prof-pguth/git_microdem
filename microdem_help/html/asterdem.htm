ASTER GDEM

Both AW3D30 and the Copernicus DEM (and even SRTM) are much better options.

 

The ASTER GDEM was created with two NIR cameras on the ASTER satellite.  One looks down at nadir, and the other is inclined in the along track direction.  After a short delay, the two image provide overlapping stereo imagery that allows creation of a photogrammetric DEM.

  • The original  DEMs are no longer freely available. 
  • The first global ASTER DEM (GDEM) became available in 2009.  Despite the 30 m (really 1") , resolution, it is probably not as good as SRTM-3" (Guth, 2010; Tomislav Hengl and Hannes I. Reuter, 2011)
  • Version 2 of ASTER GDEM came out in the summer of 2011, and was still probably inferior to SRTM 3", even with the supposedly higher resolution.   SRTM 1" is also now freely available.  Version 2 is no longer available.https://www.earthdata.nasa.gov/data/catalog/lpcloud-astgtm-002
  • Version 3 came out in August 2019, and has water masking.  It is 3601x3601, Pixel_is_Area, with one pixel overlap. https://www.earthdata.nasa.gov/data/catalog/lpcloud-astgtm-003

  • https://search.earthdata.nasa.gov/search/
    • Search for ASTER GDEM at the top left
    • Pick the area
    • Get the list of URLs you want, and save as a text file
    • You will need a NASA account, and be logged in on your browser to do the downloads (the browser will prompt you to log in for the first download).
    •   Data Manipulation, Download, Chrome list, and then pick the text file with the list of Aster tiles.  The program will open Chrome, and put each into a tab, and download.  You will have to approve each download, and it is unclear how many you can download at once (at least 17 works).  If your IT blocks this, try the IE option.
    • You can paste the URLs one at a time into the browser, in which case
    • File names look like this, so you can create your own URLs: https://e4ftl01.cr.usgs.gov//ASTER_B/ASTT/ASTGTM.003/2000.03.01/ASTGTMV003_N40W081.zip without going through the NASA site (but you will still have to log in to download)
    • Unzip the files.  There are files with the number of scenes used to get each elevation, with "num" in the file name, which you can delete.  On the Tools menu You can copy all the files ending with "dem.tif" into the correct directory, and then delete the remaing "num.tif" files.

ASTER references

ASTER DEM V4 https://www.earthdata.nasa.gov/data/catalog/lpcloud-ast14dem-004

  • search for AST14DEM
  • tiles about 75x75 km along satellite track, with missing triangles each side
  • 30 m pixel spacing
  • lots of artefacts
  • potential for time series (hundreds from DEMs over the last 20 years for a region)
