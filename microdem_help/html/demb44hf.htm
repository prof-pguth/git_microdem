DEM MEMORY ISSUES

MICRODEM requires  the DEM be in memory (RAM) because:

  • It speeds up the program considerably over disk access.
  • It simplifies coding, since there need only be different import routines and then all access of the DEM uses the program’s internal storage model.
  • DEM storage in files is not standard, even at the level of storing by row or column.
  • Many operations require a number of points surrounding the desired point (e.g. reflectance, slope, aspect), and multiple DEM formats would complicate and slow down these operations.
  • Operations like 3D oblique and perspective views, line of sight, and viewsheds require sequential points along diagonal lines and not just rows or columns.
  • The screen can display only a fraction of the points in a DEM this size. A large DEM will effectively be subsampled to display on screen.
    • Typical screen sizes are about 1000 x 1000 (now may 1920 by 128 pixels may be common, and 3840x2160 is coming up fast (4K)).
    • Even my four monitor large system is only about 4000x2000 pixels, athough I do a second 2K monitor.
  • With subset, merge, and thin options supported in the program, it should be possible to create a suitable DEM of the desired region. Small scale DEMs should cover a large area at lower resolution, while large scale DEMs would cover small areas with better resolution. It is probably appropriate to have two scales of DEM covering the same region, a small scale DEM for regional synthesis and large scale DEMs for particular areas of interest.
  • You can have a small scale overview map, and use it to pick the large scale DEM for specific areas of interest for detailed analysis.

Running out of  memory can come from two sources.  You should be able to tell which source caused the problem by looking at the error message.  If it does not include words like "DEM", "column", or "Row", it is probably being passed from the operating system to MICRODEM, and it is unlikely we can fix the problem and you will have to be less "greedy" and "smarter" in what you want to do.

  • You try to load a DEM, probably by merging, that is too big for the current limits.  You should really think about whether you really need that level of detail over such a large area (you cannot see it on screen, and probably cannot also do meaningful analysis with it).  This is less than the amount of memory on your system.
  • You try to create a map window on screen that exceeds the limits of Windows or your graphics card.  This limit has been creeping up over time, and depends on your system's characteristics, but overall I think it is plenty big (although it will be much much less than the amount of memory you have).   You should have gotten a warning that this will take a lot of memory, and noted that you cannot display it all on screen at once and would have to use scrollbars.  In this case, Subset & zoom.

With the 64 bit version the only one normally supported, and even larger DEMs could be moved into memory.

Some programs compute multiple grids, and then use those grids to compute geomorphometric parameters.  We are considering moving in that direction, but have not adopted it for general operations becasue the grids would all have to be in memory.  Having the partial grids might speed up computations, at the cost of limiting the size of the DEMs which could be used.  For computations of the SSO, MICRODEM does compute the 3 components of the normal vector to the DEM surface and retains those in memory to speed up computations, so this in effect has three additional grids in memory, at the same cost as the original DEM.
