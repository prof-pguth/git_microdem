MICRODEM Koppen Climate Classification

Meaning of Koppen letters:

  • Capital first letter, major category
  • Capital second letter, desert/steppe BW/BS, with W for German word for desert  (followed by subtropical/mid-latitude lower case h or k)
  • First lower case letter, season of drought (f, m, w, s)--"f" stands for the German word feucht or "moist" or year round precipitation; 'm" is for monsoon.
  • Second lower case letter, temperature (a-c for summer, d for Siberian winter)

The gray lines on the graph are located at critical temperatures used to divide categories:

  • 22ºC--warmest month above for a; warmest month below for b
  • 18ºC--all months above this for an A; coldest month below for a C; annual mean divides h and k
  • 10ºC--warmest month for C and D above this point; warmest above this for c
  • 0ºC--coldest month boundary between C and D (best for US)
  • -3ºC--coldest month boundary between C and D (best for Europe)

Koppen Classification follows

  • Oliver, J.E., and Fairbridge, R.W., eds., 1987, The encyclopedia of climatology: Van Nostrand Reinhold Company, New York, p.225-227.
  • except: difference between Am and Aw climates follows McKnight, T.L., 1990, Physical geography: a landscape appreciation: Prentice Hall, Englewood Cliffs, New Jersey, p.193-194.
  • except: difference between summer and winter concentration of rain follows Critchfield, H.J., 1974, General climatology (3d ed): Prentice-Hall, Englewood Cliffs, New Jersey, p.146-148.
  • May 2000: changed C and D winter/summer rain/dry season to use the 70% rule like the B climates. 

The classification uses the following assumptions:

  • all decisions use < or >, so in the case of an = might be wrong.
  • summer half year April to Sept; winter half year Oct to March (opposite in southern hemisphere) for Cs,Cf,Cw and Ds,Df,Dw
  • classification as a B or not depends heavily upon the following:
    • summer concentration of rain is Apr-Sept > 70% annual total
    • winter concentration of rain is Oct--Mar > 70% annual total
  •  ºC and cm versions of the boundaries
