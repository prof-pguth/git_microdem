Structural Geology Form

Geology options on Map Window Toolbar, or "Overlay, Geology" menu, and then  pick "Structural Geology Computations" from popup menu.  If you want the contacts on the geologic map or satellite image, start the process from that map's toolbar.

  Main Options:
  • DB: Planar Trend surface from a digitized line contact as a series of points; for most options, usethe option on the Stats menu of the data base  table display window
  • Three point: Calculate the orientation of a plane containing three selected points on the map, and show the trace on the map. Trace contact with three point problem
  • Plane contact:  enter dip and strike at a point (or use that from a three point problem or trace contact) double click on the map, and then see how the contact follows topography.  This can get multiple parallel planes.
  • Strike from map:  double click on two points on a map, and the strike will be computed and entered in the form.  If you have a scanned geologic map, this lets you get the strikes from the geologic symbols on the map. If you add the dip and dip direction, you can trace the contact.
  • Parallel contact: you need to create a deviations DEM from a planar trend surface.   Doing a three point problem will do this. You then enter a thickness, and will see the trace of a parallel plane.
  • Trace contact: digitize a linear contact.
  • Thickness to pt: you need to create a deviations DEM from a planar trend surface.   Doing a three point problem will do this.  If you pick this option, double clicking on the map will give you the thickness of between the first plane and a parallel plane.
  • End
  • Structure options

Control output:

  • Trace plane on all maps: show the plane on all open maps
  • Show plane on stereonet
  • Auto increment colors
  • Plane: pick the color and line width for the contacts.
  • Extrapolation (m): determine the distance to go.  Smaller will be faster, and can restrict the calculations to a region over which you expect planar geometry to hold.
  • Force screen redraw (removes previous lines from this operation)
  • Contact DBF


Examples:

  • Trace contact from dip and strike measured on a scanned geologic map.
  • Trace contact from dip and strike entered with keyboard. pick one point, and the orientation of the plane (e.g. N34E 23NW) and the location of the plane will be traced across the topography.
