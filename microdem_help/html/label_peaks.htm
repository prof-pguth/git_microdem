Label Peaks On Perspectives

Button on the Perspective/Panorama viewport allows you to label the peaks.

Control this with Gazetteer tab on Perspective/Fly Through Modification

  • Manually select the USGS Gazetteer for the correct state, or the NGA gazetteer for the correct country.
  • The program cannot get elevations; they are not included in the gazetteer, and the DEM elevations are not accurate enough, because the point locations are not that accurate. Consequently we cannot now determine which peaks are visible and which are masked.
  • The centers of the labels are above the features.
  • Points are processed in alphabetical order. The first processed features will be on the top line, and later features will drop down if they would overprint a previously drawn label.
  • Up to 8 rows will be used. If too many features should be included, you will be informed how many could not be listed because there is not room for their labels.

View of the White Mountains from the volcanic tableland west of Bishop, with peak labels from USGS gazetteer for California.

You can also label peaks and other features on the map, from Map Overlays,  Gazetteer  option.
