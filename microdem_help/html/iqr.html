IQR (Inter Quantile Range)

IRQ = Q3 - Q1 and is more robust than the range or standard deviation.

Hyndman and Fan (1996) discussed 9 formulations for the quantiles, and there is not a clear "best" choice.  They advocated the need for the statistical community to rally around a best choice, but that was 30 years ago and it has not happened.

Excel has two formulations for quantiles, EXC (exclude) and INC (include).  EXC is used by more online free calculating web sites, and according to Wikipedia is one of two methods available in both R and Python.  There are noticeable differences between the two Excel versions. QGIS and ArcGIS use the INC method.

MICRODEM will

  • make IRQ maps on the filter submenu on the Raster GIS menu.   You pick the window size.  This produces the same results as the EXCEL INC (and thus QGIS and ArcGIS).
  • Compute IRQ for a field in a database table, on Double clicking on field name

Reference:

  • Hyndman, R. J., & Fan, Y. (1996). Sample Quantiles in Statistical Packages. The American Statistician, vol.50, no.4, 361–365. https://doi.org/10.1080/00031305.1996.10473566 .
