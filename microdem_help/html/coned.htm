Coastal National Elevation Database (CoNED)

The USGS CoNED Project integrates disparate light detection and ranging (LiDAR) and bathymetric data sources into a common database aligned both vertically and horizontally to a common reference system.  The resolution on land is generally  much better than in the water.

https://www.usgs.gov/core-science-systems/eros/coned

1 m data

Limited coverage, but includes Chesapeake Bay

The files are very big, so consider carefully the areas you select.  You will need GDAL to use them.

https://www.usgs.gov/core-science-systems/eros/coned

Viewer and download:

  • https://topotools.cr.usgs.gov/topobathy_viewer/
  • Data download is the last button on the top above the map.
  • Pick box
  • You will get an email when it is ready to download

https://coast.noaa.gov/dataviewer/#/

  • Can do bulk download of the tiles.  There are a lot, and they are big.
  • This might be 1/9, which is slightly lower resolution, but that also makes the files much smaller
  • Can outline a subset box, put in the cart, and then have them send you an email.  You must pick the projection parameters (go UTM or geographic)
