Half Pixel Offset

  • "Same" pixel by coordinates.
    • Blue is COP, where the posting points are the center of the support area
    • Red is ALOS, where the points points are a corner of the support area
  • Dots mark the position of approximately 900 (less away for the equator, where the pixels are clearly rectantular 1 m UTM pixels in each 1 arc second pixel
  • Grids rotated slightly (stairstepping of points) since the UTM points do not match with the arc-second grid
  • Only ¼ of the points are common to “corresponding” pixels
  • Cannot easily overlay the two maps without seeing the offset shift

 

Two factors determine this, which makes marks two grid origin models, named for the SRTM and ALOS, the first 1" global DEMs to use them

  • PixelIsPoint/PixelIsArea
  • Where the nominal corner of the DEM is located, at the center or corner of a pixel

 

DEMIX dealing with the offset issue

  • Create reference DEMs to match ALOS (pixel-is-area) and COP (pixel-is-point, which also includes  FABEM, SRTM, NASADEM, and ASTER)
  • Avoid trying to do direct map comparison of ALOS and COP
  • Use half-second grids to perform direct map comparison

Pixel centroids.

  • Two 15 seconds grids (ETOPO 2024 and SRTM15+) have pixels in the same position.
  • The 1 second grids have pixels in the different positions, which is controlled by the comined effect of two choices, the pixel representation and the chosen corner location.
