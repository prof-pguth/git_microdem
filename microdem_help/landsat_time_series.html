Landsat Time Series

This will download and unzip 4 scenes in the Nile Delta of Egypt

  • Landsat 8 from mid December 2018.  This has a 15 m Pan band in addition to the 30 m bands.
  • Three scenes from Landsat 5 from 1984, 1986, and 1994.  Landsat 5 did not have a 15 m panchromatic band.
    • The 1984 scene  may have a registration error, and does not align with the others.
  • The file names shows:
    • The satellite in the first group of characters
    • The path and row in the third group of characters (when you search for data, once you find the scene you want and then want to look at additional data collections, you can filter to just get the same path and row)
      • You can get shapefiles and KML files showing the scene locations by path and row at https://www.usgs.gov/land-resources/nli/landsat/landsat-shapefiles-and-kml-files
      • https://landsat.usgs.gov/landsat_acq#convertPathRow can convert a lat/long into the path/row
    • The band number is in the last group of characters before the TIF extension (B1 to B11). There is also a quality assurance band (BQA) which you probably do not want

Open image: you can open any of the TIF files.   The program will open the scene and display a false color image.

  • Band: select multiband or single band, choose among the available bands, and adjust contrast.
  • Subset & zoom.  These scenes are about 8500x7000 pixels, and your computer might not easily handle the large images when you look at the full resolution data.
  • Set percentage data shown for raster data.  For detailed analysis or digitzing, you need to be at 100% scale.
Overlay menu, Two map interactive comparison to see differences between dates:
  • Variable opaque merge
  • Vertical swipe compare

Create NDVI maps.

Raster GIS, Multiple grid arithmetic, Difference between two grids.  Create a difference map for the 2018 data compared to the earlier dates.

Annotate Map, Create shapefile,  Line.  Digitize a stretch of the coast where you think there has been erosion/deposition on three of the maps, and overlay all the data on a single map.

  • Insure you are at 100% view (or higher), and can see the entire region on the map before you start digitizing.
  • You might want to experiment to see if you prefer stream or point digitizing mode.

Deliverables.  Write a discussion of how Landsat document change in the Nile Delta over the past 35 years.  You should have at least 4-6 graphics.

  • How has the vegetation in the delta changed in the decades leading up to 2018?  Where have the largest changes taken place?  Do you think this reflects true change, or does the season of the imagery make a difference?
  • Does the Landsat data show erosion of the delta (e.g. https://eos.org/research-spotlights/how-fast-is-the-nile-delta-sinking)?  Cite this paper using the correct format.
