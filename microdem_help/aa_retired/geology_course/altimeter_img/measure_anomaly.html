Isolating Medium Wavelength Anomalies

To look at the medium wavength anomalies, which are expected to have wavelengths of a few 100 km, we have zoomed in on the orbit.  The top figure shows the bathymetry and geoid anomaly.

There is a small anomaly in the geoid at about 9350 km.  The bottom diagram shows the long wavelength trend of the geoid with a purple line, which is becoming more negative after the peak at around 8800 km.  The vertical purple line shows the medium wavelength anomaly as only about  1 m.  The bathymetric profile below shows that the feature on the seafloor is on the order of 100 times larger than the geoid anomaly.
